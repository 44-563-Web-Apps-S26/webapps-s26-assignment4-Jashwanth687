Jashwanth Reddy Baddam Light Opera

The Gondoliers

The Mikado

Act I — Scene: A Courtyard in Ko-Ko’s Official Residence

(Ko-Ko is discovered seated on a garden bench, with a list in his hand. Pooh-Bah stands by him.)

KO-KO.
As some day it may happen that a victim must be found, I’ve got a little list— I’ve got a little list Of society offenders who might well be underground, And who never would be missed— who never would be missed!

POOH-BAH.
Indeed? That’s very interesting.

KO-KO.
There’s the idiot who praises, with enthusiastic tone, All centuries but this, and every country but his own; And the lady from the provinces, who dresses like a guy, And who “doesn’t think she dances, but would rather like to try”; And that singular anomaly, the lady novelist— I don’t think she’d be missed— I’m sure she’d not be missed!

POOH-BAH.
You are certainly rendering the State some service.

KO-KO.
And that Nisi Prius nuisance, who just now is rather rife, The Judicial humorist— I’ve got him on the list! And the idiot who thinks it is a clever thing to do To look like other people’s wives and say “At home to you”; And the advertising quack who wearies with his grin, And the music-hall singer with a caricatured chin— They’d none of ’em be missed— they’d none of ’em be missed!

POOH-BAH.
And I suppose you propose to select one of these?

KO-KO.
I am much indebted to you for your suggestion. I shall put it in hand at once.
